    SM-2 Algorithm Description

    The SM-2 algorithm is used for spaced repetition, a technique to enhance long-term memory retention. It tracks the following properties for each card:

    • Repetition number (n): Number of times the card has been successfully recalled in a row.
    • Easiness factor (EF): Indicates how "easy" the card is, starting at 2.5.
    • Inter-repetition interval (I): The time (in days) before the card is reviewed again.

    When reviewing a card, you grade your recall from 0 to 5, and the SM-2 algorithm updates n, EF, and I based on your grade.
